
Sharing the first batch of Wander State website wireframe specs for you to build in Figma. This covers:
    •    Home page (desktop + mobile)
    •    Beverages page (desktop + mobile)
These are layout + UX blueprints (not final visual design), but I’ve included enough detail that you can drop them straight into our 1440px / 375px system.

Global design assumptions
    •    Desktop canvas: 1440px
    •    Mobile canvas: 375px
    •    Grid (desktop): 12 columns, 80px side margins, 32px gutters
    •    Grid (mobile): 4 columns, 24px side margins, 16px gutters
    •    Section spacing (desktop):
    ◦    Hero: ~160px top / bottom
    ◦    Most sections: ~120px top / bottom
Typography system (high level, so you know scale):
    •    H1: large serif display (hero, big statements)
    •    H2/H3: clean sans-serif
    •    Body: 16–20px sans-serif, 1.5 line height
    •    Buttons: medium-weight sans-serif
Color-block placeholders:
    •    We’re using color blocks (not actual photos yet) for:
    ◦    Hero imagery
    ◦    Product renders
    ◦    Partner/lifestyle images
    ◦    Map / community visuals
You can treat those as image frames in Figma.

1. Home Page – Desktop (1440px)
Purpose: Lead with brand emotion → then product → then function → then community.
Hero
    •    Full-width hero block, ~760px tall.
    •    Left side:
    ◦    H1: “Stay Curious. Explore. Connect.”
    ◦    Subhead: “A sparkling tea designed to nourish your journey — from the mountains to the everyday moments.”
    ◦    Buttons:
    ▪    Primary: “Shop Beverages”
    ▪    Secondary: “Explore Our Ingredients”
    •    Right side: large image/video placeholder (PNW / nature lifestyle).
Layout:
    •    H1 + subhead aligned to left grid, roughly top third of the hero.
    •    Image block aligned to the right, taking ~60% width.

Section 2 – Tagline Block
    •    Centered H2: “Nourish Your Journey.”
    •    Supporting sentence below, max width ~640px, centered.
    •    Purpose: quick brand manifesto moment before diving into product.

Section 3 – Product Teaser (4 SKUs)
    •    Section title: something like “Sparkling Tea for Every State of Mind.”
    •    3–4 product cards in a row (slider or grid depending on dev):
    ◦    Can image placeholder
    ◦    SKU name
    ◦    Very short flavor line
    ◦    Micro CTA: “View Beverage” or “Learn More”
    •    Cards: rounded, light background, slight shadow, plenty of white space.

Section 4 – Daypart Selector
    •    Section title: “Your Daily Ritual, Reimagined.”
    •    Horizontal tab row:
    ◦    Morning Clarity
    ◦    Evening Unwind
    ◦    Movement & Endurance
    ◦    Social & Celebrations
    •    When a tab is active, show:
    ◦    Left: image placeholder (daypart visual)
    ◦    Right: title, short description, and a button (“Explore This Ritual”).
This is basically a 2-column block + a tab bar on top.

Section 5 – “Why Mushrooms” Preview
    •    3 columns, each a simple feature card:
    ◦    Lion’s Mane — Clarity
    ◦    Reishi — Calm & Balance
    ◦    Cordyceps — Endurance & Vitality
    •    Each card:
    ◦    Circle icon placeholder
    ◦    Short headline
    ◦    2–3 line description
This section is just a teaser; full explanation lives on the Mushroom Science page.

Section 6 – Clinical Evidence Preview
    •    Light background band.
    •    One “hero” quote or short statement about clinical trials, e.g.:
“Lion’s Mane has been shown in double-blind, placebo-controlled human trials to support cognitive performance.”

    •    Button: “View Clinical Research.”
This maps to the detailed science/clinical page we already planned.

Section 7 – Friends & Partners Preview
    •    2-column layout:
    ◦    Left: photo placeholder (partner / farm / forest)
    ◦    Right: copy about Monterey Nutra, Forest Farmers, WTA
    •    Button: “Meet Our Friends & Partners.”

Section 8 – Community CTA (“Where We Wander”)
    •    Full-width banner:
    ◦    Headline: “Where We Wander”
    ◦    Short line: “Share your hikes, travels, and everyday moments. Explore the Wander community map.”
    ◦    Button: “Add Your Wander.”

Footer
    •    Dark background.
    •    3 columns with:
    ◦    Navigation links (Where to Find Us, Stay Connected, Blog, etc.)
    ◦    Social icons
    ◦    Compliance text

2. Home Page – Mobile (375px)
Same sections, but stacked and simplified.
    •    Hero:
    ◦    H1 and subhead centered, buttons stacked, image below.
    •    Product teaser:
    ◦    Cards stacked vertically (full width).
    •    Daypart selector:
    ◦    Tabs horizontally scrollable, content stacked (image on top, text below).
    •    “Why Mushrooms”:
    ◦    3 cards stacked.
    •    Partners & Community:
    ◦    Each section becomes a single-column, stacked layout.
    •    Footer:
    ◦    Single column with links, then social, then compliance.

3. Beverages Page – Desktop (1440px)
Purpose: Make the SKUs craveable and clear, with an emphasis on drinkability + function.
Intro
    •    H1: “Crisp. Light. Effervescent. Crafted for How You Live.”
    •    Subhead: “Real tea. No added sugar. Low calorie. Full-spectrum functional mushrooms in every sip.”

Product Grid (4 SKUs)
    •    2 × 2 grid of product cards.
    •    Each card:
    ◦    Can image placeholder
    ◦    SKU name
    ◦    Daypart (e.g., Morning Clarity / Evening Calm / Movement / Non-Alc Aperitif)
    ◦    Short flavor description
    ◦    Button/Link: “View Beverage”
We can reuse the same card style as on the homepage, just more detailed.

Daypart Imagery Section
    •    Horizontal band or scroller that visually reinforces when/where each drink fits:
    ◦    Lemon & Mint – sunrise / bench by lake
    ◦    Chamomile & Blackberry – evening in bed with a book
    ◦    Yuzu & Cucumber – running in a forest
    ◦    Hibiscus & Blood Orange – party/social moment
These can be simple image frames for now.

Ingredient Transparency Block
    •    3-column block:
    ◦    Real Tea
    ◦    Full-Spectrum Mushrooms
    ◦    No Added Sugar / Low Calorie
    •    Each with icon + 2–3 lines of copy.

Beverages CTA
    •    Simple closing CTA:
    ◦    Headline: “Find Your Wander State.”
    ◦    Button: “Shop All Beverages.”

4. Beverages Page – Mobile (375px)
    •    Intro: H1 + subhead centered, standard mobile spacing.
    •    Product grid: cards stacked vertically.
    •    Daypart imagery: horizontal scroll or stacked images.
    •    Ingredient block: icons arranged in a 2 × 2 or 3-row layout.
    •    CTA: full-width button at bottom.
